What is technology?

Technology has a long and illustrious history. The following statement is a fundamental point: technology has been with us since the beginning of time. What exactly is technology? That is a question we hope to answer in this article.

The definition of Technology

Technology is derived from the Greek: techne - art, craft; logia - study of; the application of scientific knowledge for practical purposes, especially in industry. The preceding definition is a modern meaning.

Pastoral

William Carlos Williams, 1883 - 1963

The little sparrows
Hop ingenuously
About the pavement
Quarreling
With sharp voices
Over those things
That interest them.
But we who are wiser
Shut ourselves in
On either hand
And no one knows
Whether we think good
Or evil.
Then again,
The old man who goes about
Gathering dog lime
Walks in the gutter
Without looking up
And his tread
Is more majestic than
That of the Episcopal minister
Approaching the pulpit
Of a Sunday.
These things
Astonish me beyond words.

My Favorite Bread Recipes

White Whole Wheat Bread Recipe

This is the number one recipe to lead off my favorite home baked bread recipes. A simple hands-on recipe (yes, you will be kneading the dough by hand) that results in a delicious light whole wheat bread. The recipe is from the King Arthur Flour website. Enjoy.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup warm water
  • 2 tbs. orange juice (optional)
  • 1 tbs. vegetable oil
  • 2 tbs. brown sugar
  • 1¼ tps. salt
  • ¾ cup crushed walnuts (optional)
  • 3 cups white whole wheat flour
  • 2½ tsp. instant yeast

Directions

  1. Combine ingrediants and mix with hand utensil.
  2. Knead dough by hand until smooth and somewhat elastic.
  3. Place dough in lightly greased large bowl and cover with dish towel.
  4. Let dough rise until doubled in bulk.
  5. Deflate dough, place onto lightly floured surface and knead dough into 8 inch log.
  6. Place in a lightly greased 8½ x 4½ inch loaf pan. Cover with dish towel
  7. Let dough rise approximately 1 - 2 hours or until dough rises to rim of loaf pan.
  8. Preheat oven to 350 degrees.
  9. Bake 20 minutes.
  10. Tent lightly with aluminum foil and bake for an additional 15 to 20 minutes - unitl center is 190 degrees (Fahrenheit).
  11. Remove, turn out and place on cooling rack.
  12. Optional - take a stick of butter / margarine and lightly rub onto bread while cooling.
